## SOURCE 1 — Press Release (company-issued)

**Headline:** Nimbus Logistics Closes $84M Series B Led by Lightning Capital
**Date:** 2026-01-14
**Source:** company press wire

OAKLAND, Calif. — Nimbus Logistics, Inc., the cloud-native logistics platform for mid-market e-commerce brands, today announced the close of its $84 million Series B financing round. The round was led by Lightning Capital with participation from existing investors Sequoia Capital, Accel, and a strategic investment from FedEx Ventures.

The round values the company at $560 million post-money, more than doubling its previous valuation of $230 million from its 2023 Series A.

"We're thrilled to partner with Lightning Capital and to bring FedEx Ventures into the cap table as a strategic," said co-founder and CEO Mira Patel. "Our platform is now powering fulfillment for over 1,400 e-commerce brands, and the new capital will accelerate our expansion into international markets and AI-driven inventory optimization."

The new capital brings total funding raised to $128 million across all rounds. Nimbus reported $42 million in annualized recurring revenue (ARR) as of Q4 2025, up from $11 million a year prior. The company has 187 employees across offices in Oakland, Austin, and Berlin.

Founded in 2021 by Mira Patel and CTO Daniel Wong, Nimbus runs an integrated platform combining warehouse management, carrier orchestration, and demand forecasting. Customers include Maevia, Truant Coffee, and Brick & Cobble.

## SOURCE 2 — TechCrunch News Article

**Headline:** Nimbus Logistics Raises $84M Despite Losing Two Major Customers in 2025
**Date:** 2026-01-15
**Source:** TechCrunch (republished excerpt)

Lightning Capital's $84M bet on Nimbus Logistics, announced Tuesday, comes amid swirling questions about the startup's customer concentration and recent churn. According to two sources familiar with the company's finances, Nimbus lost two of its top-five customers in late 2025 — including its largest contract, a $4.2M ARR deal with Sundry Brands that did not renew at term in November. A second customer, fast-fashion brand Klatch, exited in December, citing migration to a competing platform.

A Nimbus spokesperson confirmed the Sundry non-renewal but characterized it as "a strategic mutual decision" and declined to comment on Klatch.

The company's $42M ARR figure, cited in its press release, includes both contracts in their final quarter. Adjusted for the December churn, current run-rate ARR is closer to $36-37 million according to one of the sources.

CEO Mira Patel told TechCrunch in an interview that the company has signed three new $1M+ ARR contracts in Q1 to date and that "net retention across our customer base of 1,400 brands remains above 110%."

Insiders say the Series B was meant to close in late 2025 at a $720M valuation but was repriced down after the Sundry non-renewal became known to the lead investor in early December.

## SOURCE 3 — Personal Blog Post (former employee)

**Headline:** What I learned in 14 months at Nimbus
**Date:** 2026-02-22
**Source:** Substack post by "Daniel R", former senior engineer at Nimbus

I left Nimbus Logistics last month after 14 months. Some of it was great, some of it wasn't, and I want to be honest about both for anyone considering joining.

**The good:**
- The technical architecture is genuinely good. The carrier-orchestration layer is some of the cleanest distributed-systems code I've worked on. Daniel Wong (CTO) is the real deal.
- Customer love is real. The mid-market brands using us truly depend on the platform; I sat in support rotations and the rapport was genuine.
- Compensation is competitive — I came in at $215K base, $50K equity vest annually, both in the top quartile for senior engineers in the Bay.

**The not-so-good:**
- High burn. We were spending around $4M/month all-in last I saw the numbers (mid-2025), against ~$3.5M/month in revenue. The new round is supposed to push us to default-alive but everyone reading the team plan knew the path required either much faster growth or significant cuts.
- Layoffs in early 2025. The company quietly cut 22 people (about 11% of the org) in February 2025, mostly in customer success and BD. Not announced publicly.
- Customer concentration was real. The top 5 customers were ~38% of ARR. This was widely understood internally; the loss of Sundry was not a shock to the team but the timing relative to the fundraise was rough.

I'd still recommend joining if you're senior — the engineering work is interesting, equity could pay out, and Mira and Daniel are credible founders. But I wouldn't recommend it to someone who needs job security in the next 18 months.

## SOURCE 4 — Internal Memo (leaked to The Information)

**Headline:** Nimbus's Internal "Path to Profitability" Memo Leaked
**Date:** 2026-03-05
**Source:** The Information (paywalled article excerpt)

The Information has obtained an internal memo from Nimbus Logistics CEO Mira Patel, dated February 28, outlining a planned restructuring intended to reach cash-flow breakeven by Q4 2026. Key points from the memo:

- A 30% reduction in international headcount, primarily affecting the Berlin office. The memo states: "We will pause new hires in EMEA and reduce existing EMEA headcount from 41 to approximately 28-30 by end of Q2."
- A renewed focus on the U.S. mid-market segment, with international expansion deferred to 2027 at earliest. Berlin office will be reframed as a "support hub" rather than a sales/expansion office.
- A pricing change: the lowest tier ("Nimbus Lite") will be sunset entirely, with affected customers (~310, mostly $5K-$20K ARR) given a 90-day notice and a path to migrate to the next-tier plan or to a competitor.
- A new $50M credit facility from Silicon Valley Bridge has been secured, providing additional runway. The memo describes this as "insurance, not operating capital."

Patel writes in the memo: "The Series B gave us 24+ months of runway at current burn. The actions in this memo extend that to 36+ months and bring us within sight of operating-cash-flow breakeven by Q4 2026, which is the trigger to begin Series C conversations from a position of strength rather than necessity."

A Nimbus spokesperson, asked for comment, declined to confirm specifics in the memo but said: "We are continuously evaluating our cost structure to deliver durable growth for our customers."
