## Detailed Notes

### 1Password Business
- **Strengths:** Best-in-class security track record, excellent developer tooling (CLI), full platform coverage including Linux, transparent pricing, SOC 2 Type 2 + ISO 27001 + PCI DSS
- **Weaknesses:** No self-hosting option, proprietary vault format, no HIPAA certification
- **Best for:** Teams prioritizing security, developer experience, and compliance

### Bitwarden Enterprise
- **Strengths:** Open source, self-hostable, lowest price among enterprise options, SOC 2 Type II + ISO 27001 + HIPAA, full platform coverage
- **Weaknesses:** Admin UX less polished than 1Password, no dark web monitoring, some advanced features require contacting sales
- **Best for:** Budget-conscious teams, teams requiring self-hosting, open-source advocates

### LastPass Business
- **Strengths:** Well-known brand, feature-rich, SSO and directory integrations
- **Weaknesses:** **MAJOR SECURITY INCIDENTS** (2022 breaches, 2025 class action settlement), pricing not publicly listed, no ISO 27001
- **Best for:** Teams that don't mind the security history and can get favorable pricing through sales

### Dashlane Business
- **Strengths:** Clean security record, user-friendly interface, SOC 2 + ISO 27001
- **Weaknesses:** **No native Linux desktop app**, pricing not publicly listed, no CLI tool, no SCIM provisioning
- **Best for:** Teams without Linux users, teams prioritizing consumer-grade UX

### Keeper Business
- **Strengths:** FedRAMP certified (unique among the five), SOC 2 + ISO 27001, dark web monitoring, clean security record
- **Weaknesses:** Developer tooling less mature than 1Password, pricing only partially public, less widely adopted in developer community
- **Best for:** Teams requiring FedRAMP compliance, government contractors
