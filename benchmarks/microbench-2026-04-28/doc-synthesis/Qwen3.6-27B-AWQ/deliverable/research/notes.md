# Working Notes — Nimbus Logistics Source Analysis

## Source-by-Source Summary

### Source 1: Press Release (2026-01-14)
- $84M Series B led by Lightning Capital
- Post-money valuation: $560M (up from $230M Series A in 2023)
- Total funding: $128M across all rounds
- ARR: $42M as of Q4 2025 (up from $11M a year prior)
- 1,400+ e-commerce brands on platform
- 187 employees across Oakland, Austin, Berlin
- Investors: Lightning Capital (lead), Sequoia, Accel, FedEx Ventures (strategic)
- Founded 2021 by Mira Patel (CEO) and Daniel Wong (CTO)
- Platform: warehouse management + carrier orchestration + demand forecasting
- Named customers: Maevia, Truant Coffee, Brick & Cobble

### Source 2: TechCrunch (2026-01-15)
- Lost two top-5 customers in late 2025:
  - Sundry Brands: $4.2M ARR, did not renew in November
  - Klatch (fast-fashion): exited December, migrated to competitor
- ARR adjusted for churn: $36-37M run-rate (vs. $42M claimed)
- Sundry non-renewal characterized by company as "strategic mutual decision"
- CEO claims 3 new $1M+ ARR contracts signed in Q1 2026
- Net retention claimed above 110%
- Series B was meant to close late 2025 at $720M valuation, repriced down to $560M after Sundry news

### Source 3: Former Employee Blog (2026-02-22)
- Author: "Daniel R", former senior engineer, 14 months tenure
- Positive: good architecture, real customer love, competitive comp ($215K base + $50K equity/yr)
- Negative:
  - Burn ~$4M/month vs ~$3.5M/month revenue (mid-2025)
  - 22 layoffs (~11% of org) in Feb 2025, mostly customer success/BD, not publicly announced
  - Top 5 customers = ~38% of ARR
  - Sundry loss not a shock internally; timing vs fundraise was rough
- Recommendation: good for senior engineers, not for those needing job security

### Source 4: Leaked Internal Memo (2026-03-05)
- CEO Mira Patel memo dated Feb 28, 2026
- Restructuring plan for cash-flow breakeven by Q4 2026
- 30% reduction in international headcount (Berlin: 41 → 28-30 by end Q2)
- International expansion deferred to 2027; Berlin reframed as "support hub"
- Nimbus Lite tier sunset: ~310 customers ($5K-$20K ARR each), 90-day notice
- New $50M credit facility from Silicon Valley Bridge ("insurance, not operating capital")
- Runway: 24+ months at current burn, extended to 36+ months with memo actions
- Goal: operating-cash-flow breakeven by Q4 2026 as trigger for Series C

### Source 5: SEC Form D (2026-01-22)
- Confirms $84M total offering, all sold
- 7 investors total
- Securities: Series B Preferred Equity
- No officer/director compensation disclosed
- Use of proceeds: "expansion, working capital, general corporate purposes"
- Three additional investors beyond the four named in press release

## Key Tensions Identified

1. **ARR discrepancy**: $42M claimed vs $36-37M adjusted for churn
2. **Valuation repricing**: $720M target → $560M actual (22% down)
3. **Customer concentration**: Top 5 = 38% of ARR; lost largest ($4.2M) in Nov 2025
4. **Burn rate**: $4M/mo vs $3.5M/mo revenue = net negative ~$500K/mo
5. **Layoffs**: 22 people cut in Feb 2025, not publicly disclosed
6. **International pivot**: Berlin office being downsized 30%, expansion deferred
7. **Product tier elimination**: Nimbus Lite sunset affecting ~310 small customers
8. **Credit facility**: $50M from Silicon Valley Bridge — "insurance" not operating capital

## Source Quality Assessment

- **Source 1 (Press Release)**: Company narrative, optimistic framing. Confirms basic facts but omits negatives.
- **Source 2 (TechCrunch)**: Journalistic, cites "two sources familiar with finances." More reliable on churn/valuation repricing.
- **Source 3 (Blog Post)**: One ex-employee's perspective. Good on culture/burn/layoffs but limited scope. Not independently verified.
- **Source 4 (Leaked Memo)**: Internal document, likely authentic given specificity. Shows management's own assessment of challenges.
- **Source 5 (SEC Form D)**: Regulatory filing, most authoritative on deal structure. Limited on business metrics.
