# Dead Ends & Abandoned Ideas

## Ideas Considered and Not Included in the Brief

### 1. Including the CEO's Q1 2026 new-contract claims
**Considered:** The CEO told TechCrunch that 3 new $1M+ ARR contracts were signed in Q1 2026, and that net retention is above 110%.
**Abandoned because:** These are management claims with no independent verification. Including them would dilute the brief's critical stance. The brief focuses on verified or cross-sourced facts. The new-contract claims are mentioned in the source quality section implicitly by noting TechCrunch's reliance on unnamed sources.

### 2. Analyzing the FedEx Ventures strategic investment more deeply
**Considered:** FedEx Ventures as a strategic investor could signal distribution partnerships or supply chain integration opportunities.
**Abandoned because:** No source provides detail on what "strategic" means in this context. Speculating about partnership terms would be fabrication. The brief mentions FedEx Ventures as part of the strong syndicate but does not over-interpret.

### 3. Including the former employee's compensation details
**Considered:** The $215K base + $50K equity/year compensation data point.
**Abandoned because:** While interesting for talent-market context, it doesn't materially affect the investment decision. The brief focuses on financial and strategic factors.

### 4. Calculating implied customer count from ARR and concentration data
**Considered:** If top 5 = 38% of ARR and total ARR = $42M, then top 5 = ~$16M, and remaining 1,395 customers = ~$26M, implying average customer ARR of ~$18.6K.
**Abandoned because:** This is a derived calculation that introduces assumptions (e.g., that all 1,400 are paying customers, that the 38% figure is precise). The brief uses the raw 38% figure without deriving further metrics.

### 5. Including the "Nimbus Lite" customer economics
**Considered:** ~310 customers at $5K–$20K ARR each represents $1.55M–$6.2M in ARR being sunset.
**Abandoned because:** The range is too wide to be meaningful, and the memo doesn't specify whether these customers will migrate to higher tiers or churn. The brief mentions the tier elimination as a restructuring action without speculating on the financial impact.

### 6. Assessing the credibility of the leaked memo
**Considered:** A full section on whether the memo is authentic.
**Abandoned because:** The memo's specificity, alignment with other sources (layoffs, churn, burn rate), and the fact that it was obtained by The Information (a reputable outlet) make its authenticity reasonably high. The brief treats it as likely authentic but notes it in the source quality section rather than dedicating space to authentication.

### 7. Including a timeline of events
**Considered:** A chronological timeline of the fundraise, customer losses, layoffs, and restructuring.
**Abandoned because:** The brief is structured around themes (red flags) rather than chronology, which is more useful for an investment decision. The timeline is implicit in the narrative.

### 8. Comparing to industry benchmarks
**Considered:** Comparing Nimbus's burn rate, customer concentration, and churn to logistics-tech industry averages.
**Abandoned because:** No internet research is permitted. Industry benchmarks would need to come from the sources, and none are provided.
