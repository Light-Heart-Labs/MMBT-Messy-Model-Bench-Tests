# ADR-001: Recommendation Stance — PASS vs. MORE DILIGENCE

**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Status:** Accepted

## Context

The brief needed a clear stance: invest, pass, or more diligence needed. The evidence presents both positive signals (strong technology, credible founders, top-tier investors) and negative signals (churn, burn, restructuring, valuation repricing).

## Decision

**PASS** — not "more diligence needed."

## Rationale

"More diligence needed" is a hedge that doesn't help the partner. The evidence is sufficient to form a view: the company is in a mid-round correction with material undisclosed risks. The specific diligence items (customer concentration, burn rate, restructuring plan) are already known from the sources. Additional diligence would confirm what we already know — it wouldn't change the recommendation.

The PASS stance is conditional: we are not saying "never invest in Nimbus." We are saying "not now, at these terms, with these unresolved risks." The brief specifies what would change our view.

## Alternatives Considered

- **MORE DILIGENCE NEEDED:** Would have required specifying exactly what diligence would change the recommendation. Since the key risks are already known, this would have been a deferral without substance.
- **INVEST WITH CAVEATS:** Would have required accepting the $560M valuation despite the repricing evidence and the churn-adjusted ARR. This would not be defensible.

# ADR-002: Treatment of the Leaked Memo

**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Status:** Accepted

## Context

Source 4 is a leaked internal memo obtained by The Information. It contains specific, actionable details about restructuring plans. The question is how to weight this source relative to others.

## Decision

Treat the memo as **likely authentic** and incorporate its facts with appropriate attribution ("according to a leaked internal memo," "the memo reveals"), but do not treat it as ground truth.

## Rationale

1. The memo's details align with other sources: layoffs (Source 3), churn (Source 2), burn rate (Source 3).
2. The memo was obtained by The Information, a reputable tech journalism outlet with a track record of sourcing internal documents.
3. The memo's specificity (exact headcount numbers, credit facility amount, timeline) is consistent with an authentic internal document.
4. The memo represents management's own assessment, which is more credible than external speculation.

## Alternatives Considered

- **Treat as unverified rumor:** Would have discarded valuable information about the restructuring plan, credit facility, and breakeven timeline.
- **Treat as ground truth:** Would have over-weighted a single source. The brief uses language like "according to" and "the memo reveals" rather than stating facts as established.

# ADR-003: Structure — Red Flags vs. Balanced Pros/Cons

**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Status:** Accepted

## Context

The brief could have been structured as a balanced pros/cons list or as a narrative with a clear recommendation.

## Decision

Lead with the recommendation, then present the positive signals briefly, then detail the five red flags, then specify conditions for reconsideration.

## Rationale

1. The partner has 5 minutes. The recommendation must be the first thing they read.
2. The positive signals are real but insufficient to overcome the negative signals. They deserve acknowledgment but not equal weight.
3. The five red flags are the decisive factors. They need detailed treatment.
4. The "what would change our view" section provides a clear path forward without hedging the recommendation.

## Alternatives Considered

- **Balanced pros/cons:** Would have given equal weight to positive and negative signals, which would not reflect the actual investment thesis.
- **Narrative without explicit structure:** Would have been harder to scan quickly.
- **Red flags first, then positives:** Would have been more dramatic but less fair. The brief acknowledges the positives first to establish credibility.
