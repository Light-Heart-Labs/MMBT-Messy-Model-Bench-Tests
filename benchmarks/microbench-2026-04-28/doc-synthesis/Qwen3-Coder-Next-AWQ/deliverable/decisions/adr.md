# ADR — Executive Brief Framing for Nimbus Logistics, Inc.

## Decision: Recommendation Stance

**Recommendation: Pass** on the follow-on investment, with clear conditions that would change the stance.

### Rationale

A brief that says “more diligence needed” without a clear recommendation is hedging. The sources present a coherent picture: the company is in a defensive, restructuring phase despite the aggressive press release narrative. The investor should be told to pass *unless* specific conditions are met.

### Framing Choice: “Pass, with Conditions”

- **Pass** as the default recommendation, because the current state (adjusted ARR $36-37M, high burn, customer churn, international retreat) does not support a bullish follow-on.
- **Conditions** that would change the stance:
  1. Net retention is *truly* >110% (not just new logos masking churn)
  2. Adjusted ARR ($36-37M) is stable and growing (not declining)
  3. The 3 new $1M+ ARR contracts are fully signed and not at risk of slippage

### Why Not “Buy” or “More Diligence”?

- **Buy** would be misleading: the sources show operational strain (burn, layoffs, customer concentration) that contradicts the press release growth narrative.
- **More Diligence** is too vague. The brief should tell the investor what to *do* and what to *verify*.

---

## Decision: Structure of the Brief

### Headline Stance
Lead with “Pass” in the first paragraph, then justify.

### Narrative Arc
1. **Stance** (Pass, with conditions)
2. **The company’s story** (press release facts)
3. **The reality check** (TechCrunch + blog + memo contradictions)
4. **Key risks** (customer concentration, burn, churn)
5. **Conditions for reconsideration**
6. **Final recommendation**

### Tone
- Calibrated: “according to,” “the company claims,” “internal memo states”
- Direct: “the adjusted run-rate ARR is $36-37M, not $42M”
- No hedging in recommendation

---

## Decision: How to Handle Contradictions

### Example: ARR Figure
- Press release says $42M
- TechCrunch says $36-37M adjusted
- Internal memo implies ~$42M (burn $4M/mo vs revenue $3.5M/mo)

**Handling:** State both numbers, cite sources, and explain the adjustment. Do not fabricate a “true” number — use TechCrunch’s source-based estimate.

### Example: International Expansion
- Press release: “accelerate expansion into international markets”
- Internal memo: “pause new hires in EMEA,” reduce Berlin from 41 to ~28-30

**Handling:** Contrast the two narratives explicitly. The memo is more credible because it’s internal and operational.

---

## Decision: Source Attribution Style

- Use `[Source 1]`, `[Source 2]`, etc. inline, as specified.
- Do not attribute general knowledge (e.g., “the company was founded in 2021”) unless it’s a specific claim.
- Attribute all metrics and contradictions.

---

## Decision: Word Count Management

- Target: ≤700 words
- Structure:
  - Stance: 50 words
  - Company story: 100 words
  - Reality check: 200 words
  - Risks: 150 words
  - Conditions: 100 words
  - Final recommendation: 50 words
  - Total: ~650 words

---

## Decision: What to Omit

- Do not repeat the press release verbatim.
- Do not include boilerplate about “logistics-tech is hot.”
- Do not invent metrics (e.g., “Gross Margin X%”) — only use what’s in sources.
- Do not include the blog author’s compensation details unless directly relevant to risk (e.g., “compensation is competitive” is not material; “burn rate $4M/mo vs revenue $3.5M/mo” is).

---

## Decision: Final Recommendation Language

**Not:** “More diligence needed.”

**Instead:** “Pass — unless the investor can verify that net retention is truly above 110%, the adjusted ARR ($36-37M) is stable and growing, and the three new $1M+ ARR contracts are fully signed and not at risk of slippage.”

This is specific, actionable, and calibrated.
