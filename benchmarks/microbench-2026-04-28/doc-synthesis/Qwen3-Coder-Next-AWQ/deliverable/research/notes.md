# Working Notes — Nimbus Logistics, Inc. Deal Evaluation

## Source Summary

### Source 1 — Press Release (Company)
- $84M Series B, led by Lightning Capital, closed Jan 14, 2026
- Post-money valuation: $560M (up from $230M in 2023)
- Total funding to date: $128M
- ARR: $42M as of Q4 2025 (up from $11M a year prior)
- 1,400 customers, 187 employees (Oakland, Austin, Berlin)
- Founders: Mira Patel (CEO), Daniel Wong (CTO)
- Investors: Lightning Capital, Sequoia, Accel, FedEx Ventures

### Source 2 — TechCrunch
- Lost two top-5 customers in late 2025:
  - Sundry Brands ($4.2M ARR) — non-renewed Nov 2025
  - Klatch (fast-fashion) — exited Dec 2025
- Company confirmed Sundry but not Klatch
- Adjusted ARR after December churn: $36-37M (per TechCrunch source)
- CEO claims 3 new $1M+ ARR contracts signed in Q1 2026
- Net retention claimed above 110%
- Series B originally intended to close at $720M valuation in late 2025, repriced down after Sundry non-renewal

### Source 3 — Former Employee Blog
- Engineering quality: high (CTO Daniel Wong is “the real deal”)
- Customer love: real (mid-market brands depend on platform)
- Compensation: competitive ($215K base + $50K equity for senior engineer)
- Burn rate: ~$4M/month all-in vs ~$3.5M/month revenue (mid-2025)
- Layoffs: 22 people (~11%) in Feb 2025, mostly in customer success & BD
- Customer concentration: top 5 = ~38% of ARR
- Recommendation: join if senior (interesting work, equity upside), not if need job security in next 18 months

### Source 4 — Leaked Internal Memo (Feb 28, 2026)
- 30% reduction in international headcount (Berlin: 41 → 28-30 by end Q2)
- Pause new hires in EMEA; Berlin reframed as “support hub”
- Sunset “Nimbus Lite” tier (~310 customers, $5K-$20K ARR each)
- 90-day migration notice to next tier or competitor
- New $50M credit facility from Silicon Valley Bridge
- Goal: cash-flow breakeven by Q4 2026
- Runway extended from 24+ months to 36+ months
- Series C conversations to begin from strength, not necessity

### Source 5 — SEC Form D
- Filed Jan 22, 2026
- $84M offered and sold
- 7 investors
- First sale: Jan 14, 2026
- Security: Series B Preferred
- Use of proceeds: expansion, working capital, general corporate purposes

## Key Tensions / Contradictions

1. **Valuation vs. Reality**
   - Press release: $560M post-money
   - TechCrunch: originally intended $720M, repriced down after Sundry non-renewal
   - Internal memo: implies urgency (runway extension to 36 months, credit facility)

2. **ARR Figure**
   - Press release: $42M
   - TechCrunch: $36-37M adjusted for December churn
   - Internal memo: burn $4M/mo vs revenue $3.5M/mo → implies ~$42M ARR (if revenue ≈ ARR)

3. **Growth Narrative vs. Operational Reality**
   - Press release: “accelerate expansion into international markets”
   - Internal memo: pause EMEA hiring, reduce Berlin from 41 to ~28-30, reframe as support hub
   - Former employee: high burn, layoffs in Feb 2025, top 5 = 38% of ARR

4. **Customer Concentration Risk**
   - Press release: 1,400 customers (broad base)
   - TechCrunch & blog: top 5 = ~38% of ARR; two top-5 lost in late 2025
   - Internal memo: sunset lowest tier (310 customers, $5K-$20K ARR each) → implies churn risk in lower tiers

5. **Runway & Path to Profitability**
   - Press release: implies aggressive growth/expansion
   - Internal memo: explicit path to cash-flow breakeven by Q4 2026, credit facility as “insurance”
   - Former employee: everyone knew path required “much faster growth or significant cuts”

## Initial Recommendation Framework

**Pro-Investment:**
- Strong engineering (CTO quality, clean architecture)
- Real customer love (mid-market dependence)
- New $50M credit facility + extended runway
- 3 new $1M+ ARR contracts signed in Q1 2026 (if true)
- Net retention >110% (if true)

**Con-Investment (Pass):**
- High customer concentration (top 5 = 38% of ARR)
- Two top-5 customers lost in late 2025
- Adjusted ARR $36-37M (not $42M)
- High burn ($4M/mo vs $3.5M/mo revenue)
- Layoffs in Feb 2025 (11% of org)
- International expansion deferred (contradicts press release)
- Net retention >110% contradicts churn data (Sundry + Klatch = ~$X+ ARR lost)

**Key Unknowns:**
- Are the 3 new $1M+ ARR contracts signed and closed? Or just term sheets?
- Is net retention >110% real, or does it include large new logos offsetting churn?
- Will the $50M credit facility be sufficient if growth slows further?
- Can Nimbus Lite customers be migrated successfully, or will it trigger broader churn?

**Stance:** **Pass** — unless investor can verify:
1. Net retention is truly >110% (not just new logos offsetting churn)
2. The $36-37M adjusted ARR is stable and growing
3. The 3 new $1M+ contracts are fully signed and not at risk of slippage

**If verified:** reconsider as “more diligence needed” → could be a turnaround story.
