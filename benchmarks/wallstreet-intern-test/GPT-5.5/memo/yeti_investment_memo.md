# YETI Holdings Investment Memo

Date: 2026-04-27 [T000]

## Recommendation

**HOLD.** YETI is a high-quality premium consumer brand, but the current price already reflects a fair amount of the cash-flow recovery I can underwrite. The stock was fetched at **$39.62** [T001] with a market cap of **$3.0B** [T002]. My **12-month price target is $41** [T003], implying **3.5% upside** [T004]. That is not enough for a Buy, and downside is not large enough for a Sell.

The core debate is simple: international growth and brand-led cash generation are real, but US Drinkware normalization, tariffs, and discretionary consumer risk keep the risk/reward balanced. I do **not** see a durable sell-side blind spot; consensus targets and estimates already look optimistic versus a guide-derived base case.

## Thesis

YETI exited FY2025 with **$1.868B revenue** [T005], **2.1% revenue growth** [T006], **$212.1M free cash flow** [T007], **11.3% FCF margin** [T008], **11.4% operating margin** [T009], and **57.4% gross margin** [T010]. Those are good economics for a discretionary consumer brand, especially with tariff pressure already visible.

The business mix is also better than a simple cooler company. FY2025 DTC sales were **$1.128B** [T011] versus wholesale sales of **$740.7M** [T012]. International sales were **$394.4M** [T013], or **21.1%** of sales [T014]. Management says its "international addressable market exceeds the US" ([Q4 transcript line 199](../extracted/transcripts/yeti-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript.txt#L199)) and describes meaningful runway for international mix to rise ([Q4 transcript line 208](../extracted/transcripts/yeti-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript.txt#L208)).

The near-term model is deliberately moderate: FY2026 revenue grows **7.0%** [T015] to **$1.999B** [T016], and FCF reaches **$219.8M** [T017]. That sits within management's FY2026 framework: adjusted EPS of **$2.77-$2.83** [T035, T036], FCF of **$200M-$225M** [T037, T038], capex of **$60M-$70M** [T040, T041], and planned repurchases of **$100M** [T039].

## Valuation

The valuation uses a **10.8% WACC** [T018] and **2.75% terminal growth** [T019]. The DCF value is **$37.89/share** [T020]. Cross-checks produce **$49.22/share** from EV/EBITDA [T021] and **$40.89/share** from P/E [T022]. The blended target weights DCF most heavily and lands at **$41/share** [T003].

## Competitive Position

YETI's brand and gross margin support a premium to weaker consumer-products peers, but not a top-peer multiple. YETI trades at **11.57x EV/EBITDA** [T031], below Acushnet's **18.92x** [T032] and above Mattel's **7.58x** [T033] and Newell's **8.77x** [T034]. That relative position looks reasonable: YETI has better brand economics than broad household-products peers, but it is still exposed to discretionary demand, wholesale inventory, and tariff volatility.

The strongest competitive evidence is consumer demand holding up better than sell-in. In Q3 FY2025, management described a "notable gap compared to very strong double digit sell through" ([Q3 transcript lines 91-93](../extracted/transcripts/yeti-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript.txt#L91)). That supports the idea that retailer caution is masking some end-demand resilience, but it is not enough to call the stock mispriced.

## Sell-Side / Mispricing View

I tested the required "what is sell-side missing?" angle and came away unconvinced. Public consensus target data show an average target of **$47.75** [T027] and median target of **$53.00** [T028], both above my target. Consensus FY2026 revenue is **$2.038B** [T029] and consensus EPS is **$2.87** [T030], above the guide-derived base case and above the high end of management's adjusted EPS range of **$2.83** [T036].

So the market price, not the sell-side target, is the skeptical input. My read is that the stock is fairly efficient: investors are discounting tariff uncertainty, US category softness, and execution risk while sell-side models already credit international growth and recovery.

## Key Risks

Tariffs are the largest near-term swing factor. Management said the FY2026 guide embeds "approximately 200 basis points" of incremental tariff impact ([Q4 transcript lines 372-374](../extracted/transcripts/yeti-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript.txt#L372)) [T042]. They also framed tariff cost versus FY2024 at **$80M** and **430 bps** [T043]. If tariff relief comes faster than modeled, upside exists; if mitigation stalls, gross margin and EPS estimates are vulnerable.

US Drinkware and wholesale inventory are the second risk. The model assumes FY2026 revenue growth of **7.0%** [T015], but that depends on international growth and product innovation offsetting a still-competitive US category. DTC helps, yet wholesale remains large at **$740.7M** of FY2025 sales [T012].

Capital allocation is a modest support, not a thesis by itself. The model includes the planned **$100M** FY2026 repurchase [T039], but repurchases cannot offset a brand or category disappointment.

## Confidence And Limitations

I am most confident in the historical financials, sales mix, guidance ranges, and market inputs because they trace directly from raw files through extraction artifacts into the workbook and trace table. I am less certain about the WACC, terminal growth, and multiple selection because those are judgment calls; the discount-rate and valuation ADRs explain the choices.

A human analyst would add paid sell-side model detail, channel checks, retailer sell-through data, and a more granular tariff bill-of-materials sensitivity. Within the public evidence gathered here, the cleanest recommendation is **HOLD** with a **$41** price target [T003].
