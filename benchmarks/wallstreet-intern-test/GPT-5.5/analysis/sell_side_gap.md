# Sell-Side Miss / Mispricing Test

Date: 2026-04-27

## Question

Is there a credible thing the sell-side appears to be missing on YETI?

## Evidence Checked

- Public consensus is not low: the fetched consensus target is $47.75 average and $53.00 median versus the model's $41 target and the fetched $39.62 current price. See trace rows T001, T003, T027, and T028 in `analysis/memo_trace_table.csv`.
- Public consensus is above the 2026 guide-derived model: consensus 2026 revenue is $2.038B and consensus EPS is $2.87. Management's FY2026 sales guide implies 6%-8% adjusted sales growth, and the model uses the 7% midpoint for $1.999B revenue. Management's adjusted EPS guide is $2.77-$2.83. See trace rows T015, T016, T029, T030, T035, and T036.
- International growth is real but not hidden. Management said its international addressable market exceeds the US and repeatedly framed international as a long-term driver in the Q4 2025 transcript. See `extracted/transcripts/yeti-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript.txt` lines 199-208 and trace rows T013-T014.
- Tariff mitigation is also not hidden. Management quantified about 200 bps of incremental 2026 tariff impact and about $80M of tariff cost versus 2024. See trace rows T042-T043.

## Conclusion

I do not see a durable sell-side miss in the public data. The most tempting upside angle is international scale, but it is explicitly highlighted by management and already reflected in consensus revenue above the guide-derived base case. The stock looks closer to efficiently priced: sell-side targets are higher, while the market price appears to discount tariff uncertainty, US category softness, and execution risk.

## How This Affects The Recommendation

The memo uses a `HOLD` recommendation. A `BUY` would require either evidence that tariffs are materially over-discounted, a cleaner US Drinkware recovery, or international growth that is not already embedded in consensus.
