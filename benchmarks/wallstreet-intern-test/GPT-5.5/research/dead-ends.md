# Dead Ends

## Investor-Day / Long-Range Deck

I looked for a current investor-day or long-range planning deck to anchor a management algorithm. The initially plausible investor-day URL pattern did not resolve to a usable current deck, and search results did not surface a better company-hosted replacement. I used the 2026 proxy, filings, earnings releases, and transcripts instead.

## Solo Brands As A Core Peer

Solo Brands (`DTC`) looked like a potentially close outdoor/DTC peer. The fetched market data showed a very small market cap and negative operating margin, making it a distressed comparator rather than a useful core comp. I retained the raw peer file for audit but excluded `DTC` from the core peer decision in ADR 0003.

## Sell-Side Blind Spot

I tested whether international growth or tariff mitigation represented a public sell-side miss. Both are clearly discussed by management, and public consensus revenue/EPS are already above the guide-derived base case. I therefore did not force a `BUY` thesis; the analysis is in `analysis/sell_side_gap.md`.
