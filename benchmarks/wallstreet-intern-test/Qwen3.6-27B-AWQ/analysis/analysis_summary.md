# GitLab Inc. (GTLB) - Investment Analysis

## Overview
This notebook contains the analytical work supporting the investment memo on GitLab Inc.

## Key Findings

### 1. Revenue Growth Trajectory
GitLab has demonstrated consistent revenue growth:
- FY2023: $424M (+41% YoY)
- FY2024: $580M (+37% YoY)
- FY2025: $759M (+31% YoY)
- FY2026: $955M (+26% YoY)

Growth is moderating but remains strong for a company approaching $1B in revenue.

### 2. Path to Profitability
- Operating margins improving: -50% → -32% → -19% → -7%
- FY2026: Operating loss of $70M on $955M revenue (-7.4%)
- Adjusted operating income (adding back SBC) is positive
- Projected to reach GAAP operating profitability in FY2028

### 3. SaaS Metrics
- ARR: ~$860M (FY2026)
- Net Revenue Retention: ~115% (declining from 120% in FY2022)
- Gross Margin: 87.4% (excellent for SaaS)
- Deferred Revenue: $572M (strong visibility into future revenue)

### 4. Competitive Position
- Unique full-stack DevOps platform
- Open-core model provides distribution advantage
- Competing with Atlassian, GitHub (MSFT), and point solutions
- AI/ML features (Gitter, AI code review) as differentiation

### 5. Valuation
- Current: $21.51/share, $3.66B market cap
- DCF Base Case: $38.52/share (79% upside)
- EV/Revenue Peer Avg: $53.25/share (147% upside)
- Bear Case: $18.58/share (-14% downside)
- Bull Case: $88.30/share (310% upside)
- Probability-Weighted: $45.98/share (114% upside)

### 6. What the Market May Be Missing
The market appears to be underpricing GitLab's:
1. **AI monetization potential:** GitLab's AI features (code review, security scanning) are embedded in the workflow, creating natural upsell paths
2. **ARR quality:** 115% NRR with $860M ARR provides $99B in 10-year revenue run-rate if maintained
3. **Balance sheet strength:** $1.26B in cash/investments with negligible debt provides optionality
4. **Operating leverage:** As revenue grows, fixed costs (R&D, platform) spread over larger base

## Sell-Side Consensus
- Mean price target: ~$30-35 (based on yfinance data)
- Most analysts focus on near-term profitability timeline
- Limited analysis of AI monetization potential
- NRR decline from 120% to 115% seen as negative, but still strong

## Key Risks
1. **NRR decline:** From 120% to 115% over 4 years
2. **Competition:** GitHub Copilot, Atlassian AI features
3. **Open-source cannibalization:** Self-hosted users may not convert to paid
4. **Macro sensitivity:** Enterprise software spending cuts
5. **Execution risk:** AI features may not monetize as expected
