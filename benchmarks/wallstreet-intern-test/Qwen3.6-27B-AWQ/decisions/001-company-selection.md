# ADR-001: Company Selection

**Date:** 2025-06-18
**Status:** Accepted

## Context
Need to select a publicly traded US company with market cap between $1B and $10B for investment memo.

## Candidates Evaluated
| Ticker | Name | Market Cap | Notes |
|--------|------|-----------|-------|
| CELH | Celsius Holdings | $8.89B | Beverage, high growth but volatile |
| GTLB | GitLab | $3.66B | SaaS, clear metrics, good data |
| DOCS | Doximity | $4.52B | Healthcare SaaS, niche market |
| ASAN | Asana | $1.52B | Productivity SaaS, struggling |
| RXRX | Recursion | $1.85B | Biotech/AI, high uncertainty |
| AI | C3.ai | $1.26B | AI software, speculative |
| UPST | Upstart | $3.21B | Fintech, cyclical |
| BROS | Dutch Bros | $7.31B | Coffee chain, consumer |
| HIMS | Hims & Hers | $6.97B | Telehealth, regulatory risk |
| ENVX | Enovix | $1.44B | Battery tech, pre-revenue scale |

## Decision
**Selected: GitLab Inc. (GTLB)**

## Rationale
1. **Clear business model:** SaaS subscription revenue with predictable metrics (ARR, NRR, etc.)
2. **Good data availability:** Regular SEC filings, earnings calls, and public metrics
3. **Interesting competitive dynamics:** Competing with Atlassian, GitHub, and self-hosted alternatives
4. **Market cap in range:** ~$3.66B, well within the $1B-$10B target
5. **Recent price action:** Stock has declined significantly from highs, creating potential mispricing opportunity
6. **SaaS metrics are auditable:** Can trace ARR, NRR, and other key metrics directly from filings

## Alternatives Considered
- **CELH:** Rejected due to high volatility and consumer discretionary risk
- **DOCS:** Rejected due to niche market making competitive analysis harder
- **ASAN:** Rejected due to struggling business making thesis harder to construct
- **HIMS:** Rejected due to regulatory uncertainty in telehealth
