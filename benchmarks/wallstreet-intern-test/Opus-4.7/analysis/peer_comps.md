## Key observations

1. **COCO is the most-expensive name in this peer set on EV/EBITDA TTM (33.4x).** It also has the lowest EBITDA margin in the peer set (15.4% vs 14.6%–22.7%). The multiple premium implies the market expects substantially higher growth and/or margin expansion than the peers.

2. **The multiple-compression precedent is real.** Celsius traded above 50x EV/EBITDA at peak. As topline growth decelerated from triple-digits in 2023 to a single-digit run-rate by mid-2025, the multiple compressed to 16x — a >60% compression. Same trajectory in BRBR's history.

3. **Forward EV/EBITDA matters more than TTM here.** Using management's FY26 EBITDA guide midpoint of $125M:
   - COCO forward EV/EBITDA = $2.80B / $125M = **22.4x**
   - Even on the forward, COCO sits 6x above CELH, 13x above BRBR.

4. **The growth-rate justification.** COCO is genuinely growing faster than BRBR or SMPL. Vita Coco grew 18% in FY2025 and is guided to 13% in FY2026. CELH grew ~25% in 2025. BRBR is single-digit. So COCO probably *deserves* a premium — the question is how big a premium.

5. **The "deserves 22x?" question.** A reasonable rule of thumb: forward EV/EBITDA roughly equals 2-stage growth (next year + steady-state) modeled as a perpetuity. At 9% WACC, 22x EBITDA implies the market is paying for ~12% perpetual EBITDA growth. COCO's FY26 guide is 25% EBITDA growth (off a $98M base to $125M); FY27 sell-side consensus implies ~16% growth. By FY28-29, growth should normalize to category rate (~10%) plus modest share gains (1-2%) = mid-teens at best.

   So 22x is roughly appropriate today, but as the company executes through FY27 and the growth profile becomes more visible, the natural exit multiple is closer to **16-18x** — Celsius-today level rather than Celsius-peak level.

## Implication for valuation

- Sell-side consensus 12-month price target of $57.75 implicitly requires the multiple to hold at ~22x through FY27.
- Mature-peer (BRBR/SMPL) compression to 9-10x would imply a price target of $25-30. This is the implicit Bear case.
- The CELH-style decel trajectory (16x by FY27) implies a Base-case price around $43-46.
- A "category-leader keeps premium" view (20x) implies $52-55.

Our Base case uses **17x exit multiple**, midway between the BRBR/SMPL floor and the current trading multiple, reflecting the most likely path of gradual multiple compression as growth normalizes.
