Earnings Call Transcript

Vita Coco (COCO) Q2 2025 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 21, 2026 at 12:14PM EST

Image source: The Motley Fool.

DATE

Wednesday, July 30, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS

  • Executive Chairman — Michael Kirban
  • Chief Executive Officer — Martin F. Roper
  • Chief Financial Officer — Corey Baker

TAKEAWAYS

  • Net Sales -- $169 million, up 17%, driven by 25% growth in Vita Coco Coconut Water and 102% growth in other products, partially offset by a 25% decline in private label sales.
  • Americas Segment -- Vita Coco Coconut Water increased 22% to $120 million, while private label sales decreased 37% to $15 million, fueled by a 21% volume gain for Vita Coco Coconut Water and a 34% volume decline in private label.
  • International Segment -- Net sales increased 37%, with Vita Coco Coconut Water up 43% and private label up 29%, reflecting strong category and brand performance in Europe.
  • Gross Profit -- $61 million, increasing by $3 million, with a gross margin of 36%, down approximately 450 basis points due to higher ocean freight, cost inflation, and the new 10% baseline tariff, partially offset by favorable product mix.
  • Net Income -- $23 million, or $0.38 per diluted share, compared to $19 million or $0.32 per share, driven by higher gross profit, larger unrealized gain on derivatives, and a lower 19% effective tax rate.
  • Adjusted EBITDA -- $29 million (17% of net sales), down from $32 million (22% of net sales) in 2024, primarily due to increased SG&A, with SG&A up $7 million to $36 million from higher marketing, people costs, bad debt reserves, and overlapping office rent.
  • Inventory Position -- "Significantly more Vita Coco Coconut Water inventory than at the same time last year," enabling anticipated strong Q3 branded performance and more promotional opportunities.
  • Guidance Updates -- Full year net sales guidance raised to $565 million-$580 million, gross margin expected at approximately 36%, SG&A to grow low- to mid-single digits, and adjusted EBITDA projected at $86 million-$92 million.
  • Vita Coco Treats -- National rollout in the U.S. and U.K. occurred at the end of Q1, contributing incremental branded growth; Circana data indicates Treats would have added 4% to second quarter U.S. retail scan growth if consolidated with Coconut Water.
  • Private Label Outlook -- Q2 reflects the known impact of lost regions; new private label business secured will benefit results in 2026, while near-term performance is expected to remain volatile due to past inventory issues and recent contract changes.
  • Tariff Exposure -- Currently operating under a 10% baseline U.S. coconut water tariff, impacting about 60% of global cost of goods sold, with ocean freight and tariff costs partially offset by pricing actions; forward guidance does not include increases above 10%.
  • Cash & Liquidity -- Cash on hand of $167 million and no debt under the revolving credit facility as of June 30, 2025.

RISKS

  • Gross margin declined from 41% to 36%, explicitly attributed to "higher year-on-year ocean freight rates, higher finished goods product costs and the 10% baseline tariff."
  • Management acknowledged that "With U.S. tariff outcomes uncertain, we expect short-term volatility in ocean freight rates," signaling cost structure risk for the remainder of the year.
  • Adjusted EBITDA declined year over year (from $32 million to $29 million) due to elevated SG&A expenses.
  • Near-term private label sales are expected to remain "turbulent on a comparable basis for the balance of the year," reflecting ongoing volatility and limited visibility.

SUMMARY

The Vita Coco Company (COCO +3.19%) emphasized robust branded sales growth in both the Americas and internationally, supported by increased inventory and expanded product innovation, particularly from the national launch of Vita Coco Treats. The company raised full-year net sales guidance and confirmed expected gross margin and adjusted EBITDA ranges, yet noted ongoing gross margin compression due to higher input costs, ocean freight, and tariffs. Tariff exposure affecting roughly 60% of cost of goods sold led to concurrent U.S. price increases, but additional tariffs above 10% remain an uncertainty not factored into current forecasts. Private label sales are in a transition period with known declines offset by future new business, positioning 2026 as a potential inflection for that segment. Management highlighted cash strength and reiterated a focus on building household penetration and driving category growth rather than shifting away from core coconut water offerings.

  • CEO Roper said, "Our Walmart trends improved slightly during the quarter, but were still down high single to low double digits, creating an estimated low single-digit drag on our total U.S. branded scan trends," clarifying the current drag and future opportunity as Walmart prepares new resets.
  • Management stated, "We are currently executing U.S. retail price increases to cover the unmitigated tariff costs of this baseline rate," suggesting the company's willingness to pass cost increases to consumers in response to trade pressures.
  • Roper explained, "Our branded scan results in the United States were very strong, although slightly behind category growth due to the drag in our scans, primarily created by the changes in the Walmart set late last year."
  • Baker confirmed, "The largest was marketing spend in the first half, and that is a combination of the Treats, the rollout [indiscernible] a bit of timing overall in the full year marketing. And then the people costs, as I discussed earlier, led by international, second largest bucket as well as some incentive comp and stock comp items that ultimately would be adjusted out of the EBITDA."
  • Kirban emphasized, "We believe that the coconut water category is still in its infancy in markets outside of the U.S. and that there is significant long-term potential as these markets develop."
  • Management described category expansion as coming from "both growth in households and growth in velocity per household through consumption occasion expansion."

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Circana: Retail industry data provider referenced for sales and scan data.
  • SKU: Stock Keeping Unit; individual product item or variant tracked for inventory and sales.
  • Private Label: Products manufactured by The Vita Coco Company for retail customers to sell under their own brands.
  • ACV: All Commodity Volume; measures distribution reach in retail as a percentage of total available sales volume.
