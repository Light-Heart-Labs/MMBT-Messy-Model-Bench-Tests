# Thesis in three sentences

Vita Coco is the dominant U.S. coconut water brand in a category structurally growing 15-20% annually, with a credible international expansion runway and best-in-class working-capital characteristics. The fundamentals are not the problem — fundamentals are good and likely to remain good. The problem is valuation: at 33x trailing EV/EBITDA (22x forward), the stock is priced for growth to *not* decelerate, when the model says growth decelerates from +18% in FY25 to +11% by FY28, and peer history (Celsius) shows that decelerating beverage brands compress 3-6x of EBITDA multiple even while EBITDA continues compounding.

# Variant view: the multiple compresses

Sell-side consensus 12-month target is **$57.75** (8 analysts; 0 Sell ratings; tight range $54–$60). Decomposed, the consensus implies **22-23x forward EV/EBITDA at exit** — essentially today's multiple, held flat through FY27.

The peer table tells a different story:

| Ticker | Profile (2026-04-27) | EV/EBITDA TTM |
|---|---|---:|
| COCO | Pure-play, +18% TTM growth, decelerating | **33.4x** |
| CELH | Was high-growth, now decelerating | 16.1x |
| BRBR | Mature single-product (BellRing) | 9.2x |
| SMPL | Mature low-growth (Atkins/Quest) | 6.6x |

Celsius (CELH) is the cleanest historical analog. CELH peaked above 50x in 2024; as growth normalized from triple-digits to ~25%, the multiple compressed to 16x — over 60% multiple compression in <18 months despite continuing positive growth. The same path on COCO produces our Base case at 17x exit.

The variant view, expressed plainly: **a beverage company that decelerates from +18% growth to +11% over three years should not finish that period trading at the same multiple it started at.** Sell-side is implicitly making that bet. We're betting against it.

# Company snapshot

The Vita Coco Company (NASDAQ: COCO) is a pure-play coconut water brand, founded 2004, IPO'd Oct 2021. Coconut water is **96% of FY2025 revenue** [10-K MD&A, [extracted/mda_FY2025_10K.txt:44](../extracted/mda_FY2025_10K.txt)]; it is also the largest co-packer/private-label supplier in the category. Two reportable segments (Americas, International) and three product groups (Vita Coco branded coconut water, Private Label, Other — primarily the Treats coconut milk line).

# Why fundamentals are not the worry

**Coconut water category is structurally healthy.** Per Grand View Research, global coconut water was $5.1B in 2025 with an 18.2% CAGR forecast through 2033 [[sources.md M7]](../sources.md). Management cites Circana data of 22% category growth in the U.S., 32% in U.K., over 100% in Germany in 2025 [Q4 2025 call, [transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt:13](../extracted/transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt)]. This is not a category in trouble.

**Vita Coco is the U.S. category leader.** ~50% U.S. share. The retail acceleration in late 2024 / 2025 was driven by category not share — but the brand also had ~6% top-line tailwind from a Walmart shelf reset that increased its facings and moved to a higher-traffic aisle [Q4 2025 call, [transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt:22](../extracted/transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt)].

**International runway is real and accelerating.** International grew +37% in FY25 (+43% on the branded line). Germany grew >200% on the branded line in retail dollars [Q4 2025 call, [transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt:13](../extracted/transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt)]. Per-capita consumption in Europe is "five to ten years behind the U.S." per management [Q4 2025 call, [transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt:46](../extracted/transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt)] — this is a multi-year compounding opportunity.

**The asset-light model is differentiated.** Vita Coco doesn't own factories. Co-packers in Mexico, U.S., U.K. partner with coconut processors in Philippines, Brazil, Thailand, Vietnam, Malaysia, Sri Lanka, Indonesia [10-K, raw/filings/10-K_2025-12-31_26-000022.htm; cited at extracted/mda_FY2025_10K.txt sourcing section]. This affords supply diversification and structurally lower capex (FY25 capex = $8M, 1.3% of sales).

**FY2026 guidance is achievable.** Net sales $680-700M (+13% mid), GM ~38%, Adj EBITDA $122-128M [Q4 2025 call, [transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt:34](../extracted/transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt)]. Tariff exemptions secured Nov 2025 should restore most of the GM hit; only ~$2M of carry-over tariff cost remains in inventory and rolls through Q1 2026. Our Base FY26 model produces $127M EBITDA — within management's range.

# Where the worry is

### 1. Customer concentration

| Customer | FY23 | FY24 | FY25 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Customer A | 30% | 25% | **19%** |
| Customer B | 23% | 23% | **25%** |
| Combined | 53% | 48% | **44%** |

*Source: 10-K Note 12 (Concentration), citation in extracted/mda_FY2025_10K.txt. Customers are unnamed in filings.*

Customer A's share has fallen from 30% to 19% over three years, reflecting lost private-label regions. Customer A "has requested to restart supply in early 2026 for one of those lost regions" per the 10-K [extracted/mda_FY2025_10K.txt:53-54], but there's no contractual visibility beyond that. The combined top-2 = 44% remains uncomfortable for a $3B-cap brand.

### 2. Multiple compression risk

Detailed in `analysis/variant_view.md`. Summary: COCO trades at >2x the EV/EBITDA multiple of its closest decelerating peer (CELH at 16x) and >3x the multiple of mature peers (BRBR/SMPL at 9-10x). The premium is justifiable today on growth and category leadership; sustaining a 22x forward multiple over 18 months requires growth to *re-accelerate* or hold, neither of which our model assumes.

### 3. Pricing has done a lot of work

FY25 Americas Vita Coco net pricing improvement was +5% on top of +19% volume growth. Two price increases were taken in 2025 to absorb tariff costs; management has guided to "low single digit" net price growth for FY26 [Q4 call [transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt:35](../extracted/transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt)]. As pricing comps lap and promotional intensity rises ("we have planned increase in promotional initiatives so that we remain competitive" — same line), the volume-vs-price mix becomes less margin-friendly.

### 4. International is small

Yes, +37% growth is real. But the base is $101M of FY25 sales — even at +35% sustained for three years, International reaches only $250M by FY28. The Americas business is what determines the multiple, and Americas grew 15% in FY2025 (and is decelerating per our model).

New private-label wins materialize beyond guidance. Tariff relief sticks. GM expands to 40%+ structurally. Multiple holds at 22x as the market continues to price COCO as a high-growth beverage category leader. Stock at $76.

# Catalysts

**Positive:** Q1 2026 results (late April / early May) — first full quarter showing tariff relief and Walmart reset benefit. International segment reporting at >40% growth. New private-label wins announced. M&A (~$200M cash burns hole; management has flagged it).

**Negative:** Q1 2026 results miss tariff-relief expectation. Customer concentration disclosure shows further Costco region attrition. Coconut commodity inflation. Tariff returns as policy. Competitive entry (CELH coconut launch; KO/PEP brand push).

# Confidence and limitations

**What I'm confident in:**
- The fundamental data (revenue, segment splits, gross margins). All traced to specific filings via `extracted/annual.csv` + `extracted/segment_history.csv`.
- The peer-multiple trajectory. Empirical: CELH compressed 60% as growth slowed.
- Management is competent. The Q4 commentary on tariff offsets, Walmart reset, and international expansion is concrete and consistent.

**What I'm guessing on:**
- The right exit multiple. 17x base is defended in `analysis/peer_comps.md` but it's a judgment call; reasonable analysts could land at 15x or 20x.
- Probability weights (25/50/25). I tried to be honest. A skeptic could argue 35/45/20 (more bearish) or 15/55/30 (more bullish).
- The non-operating income forecast. FY25 had +$10M from FX gains that I project to +$5M in Base and $0 in Bear. This is a material wildcard for EPS but doesn't move EBITDA.

**What a human analyst would do that I didn't:**
- Speak to private-label customers directly (Costco buyer relationships, retail operations contacts) to validate Customer A trajectory.
- Pull Circana/Nielsen scanner data weekly to monitor Walmart-reset trajectory. Public investors don't have this; sell-side does.
- Build a coconut commodity model (green coconut prices in Philippines, Vietnam, Brazil) to size 2026 GM headwind risk separately from the tariff narrative.
- Talk to industry players about the GLP-1 impact on coconut water (anecdotally, hydration is a beneficiary, but no hard data).
- Construct a scanner-data based "running 12-week growth" tracker to identify the moment when category growth decelerates.

**What could most surprise me:**
- International accelerates faster than the bull case. If Germany hits +200% on the branded line for one more year, the bull thesis becomes Base.
- Costco fully terminates the relationship. That's a -$30M revenue hit at low margin, manageable but headline-negative.
- Aggressive M&A. Vita Coco has $200M cash and an itch to deploy it. A poorly-priced acquisition would be the largest unmodeled risk to the Bull thesis.
