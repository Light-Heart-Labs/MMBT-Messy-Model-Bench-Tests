# Questions Log

Every question that came up during research, and how it was resolved.
Open questions stay at the top; resolved questions follow.

## Open

- **Who is "Customer A" exactly?** The 10-K discloses Customer A and Customer B without naming them. From context (private label coconut water; lost regions in 2024-25; restarting one region in 2026; previously a coconut oil supplier), Customer A is widely believed to be Costco (Kirkland brand). I have not independently confirmed this from a primary source. The investment thesis doesn't require knowing the name (the disclosed concentration trajectory tells the story), but a reader should understand this is inferred, not cited. Status: open, low-priority.
- **What % of Vita Coco's "branded coconut water" volume passes through DSD distribution from Keurig Dr Pepper vs. direct-to-retailer?** The 10-K mentions DSD partners (line 35-ish in mgmt commentary on Q4 2025 call: distributor inventory pull-forward of $7M in Q4). The customer that "acquired less than 5% ownership... at the time of the IPO" is implied to be a strategic partner (KDP-like). Not material to the model but would inform competitive risk if KDP relationship changed. Status: open, low-priority.
- **What's actually driving the 4% net pricing improvement in FY25 Americas Vita Coco?** Two price increases were taken (per Q4 call) but the net realized pricing was lower than the sticker increases due to trade promotion. Bridging the gap from sticker to net is a black box. Status: cannot be resolved from disclosure; open and acknowledged.

## Resolved

- **Does the FY2025 10-K disclose customer concentration?** Yes. Customer A: 19% of FY25 sales (from 30% in FY23 and 25% in FY24). Customer B: 25% of sales (steady at 23-25% over 3 years). Combined top 2 = 44% of FY25 sales. Resolved by direct text search in `extracted/mda_FY2025_10K.txt` and the underlying 10-K (`raw/filings/10-K_2025-12-31_26-000022.htm`).
- **Is the FY2025 GM decline (38.5% → 36.5%) all about tariffs?** Approximately yes. Of the 200bp decline, ~200bp came from tariff costs ($14M / $610M ≈ 2.3pp). That implies tariff-removal in FY26 should put GM back to FY24 levels (38-39%) all else equal. Cross-checks the management guide of ~38% GM. Resolved via Q4 call transcript line 29 and 10-K MD&A.
- **What's the right exit multiple to use for FY27?** A range. 22x = current trading multiple; 16x = Celsius-decelerated; 9-10x = mature peer (BRBR/SMPL). Base case uses 17x as the most likely landing zone. Reasoning lives in `analysis/peer_comps.md` and `analysis/variant_view.md`.
- **Does management buy back stock?** Yes, modestly. $11.3M repurchases in FY2025; $40.9M remaining on a $65M authorization. Source: Q4 2025 press release table 1 (`extracted/press_tables/PR_Q4-FY2025_26-000019__table_01.csv` does not show this directly but the press release narrative does — see `raw/filings/PR_Q4-FY2025_26-000019.htm` in the "Balance Sheet" paragraph).
- **Is private label growing in FY26 or shrinking?** Growing per management guidance. Q4 2025 call (Corey Baker): "We expect strong private label net sales growth of 20% to 25% in the U.S. as we regain some geographic regions at multiple retailers and launch a new one as previously discussed." `extracted/transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt:36`.
- **What's the FY2026 EBITDA guide?** $122-128M. Source: Q4 FY2025 press release and earnings call (`extracted/transcripts/Q4-FY2025_call.txt:34`). Mid-point $125M. My Base FY26 model produces $127M — within the guide.
- **What's tariffs' net impact in FY2026?** Mostly removed. Tariff exemption for coconut water products effective Nov 14, 2025; Brazil incremental tariff removed Nov 21, 2025. ~$2M of tariffs already paid in FY2025 will roll through inventory into Q1 2026 P&L. After Q1, "long-term cost of goods representative of our ongoing business" should kick in. Source: Q4 call transcript line 24, 10-K MD&A (`extracted/mda_FY2025_10K.txt:33-35`).
- **Is the recent stock run justified by fundamentals?** Mostly yes — but with multiple expansion as the single largest contributor. Stock went from ~$30 to $52 in 12 months (+75%); FY26 EBITDA forecast went from ~$100M to $125M (+25%). The other +50% came from multiple expansion (TTM EV/EBITDA from ~22x to 33x). That's the variant-view setup.
- **Why does the company report Vita Coco Coconut Water vs Private Label vs Other separately?** Different competitive dynamics, different customer concentration, different gross margin. Per 10-K MD&A and segment notes. Reflected in the model: 6 separate revenue lines in the IS.
- **Is the Walmart reset already in the run-rate?** Mostly yes for FY26 but lapping starts Nov 2026. Per management Q4 call line 22 and Q1 2026 implied data: "Walmart is adding 5% to 6% to our total scans right now" — that lift will fully embed in FY26 results, then lap to flat in late FY27.
