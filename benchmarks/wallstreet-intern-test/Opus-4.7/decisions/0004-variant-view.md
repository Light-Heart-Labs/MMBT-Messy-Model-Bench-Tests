# ADR-0004: Variant view — multiple compression is the mispricing

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-04-27

## Context

The brief asks the analyst to "find at least one thing the sell-side appears to be missing or mispricing. If you can't, say so and explain why the stock is efficiently priced." This ADR records the variant view I landed on, the alternatives I considered, and why I picked this one.

## Decision

**The variant view: COCO trades at 33x TTM / 22x forward EV/EBITDA. Sell-side consensus implicitly assumes that multiple holds through FY27. I think the multiple compresses to ~17x as growth decelerates, taking the stock to ~$46 from $52.39.**

The variant claim is *not* about the operating numbers. The company will probably hit FY26 guide. Numbers are not where the disagreement is.

The variant claim is purely about valuation: a beverage company decelerating from +18% growth to +11% over three years should not trade at the same multiple at the end of that deceleration as at the start. That's a statement about how multiples respond to growth profile, anchored in observed peer trajectories (Celsius compressed >60% as growth normalized).

## Variant-view candidates considered

I considered five candidate variant views. Each is plausible; I chose multiple compression because it has the cleanest empirical support.

1. **"Tariff savings tailwind."** Tariffs cost $14M expensed in FY25. Removed by Nov 2025; full-year benefit in FY26. Sell-side may anchor on average historical GM and miss the tariff recovery.
   - Rejected as primary because management explicitly guided to ~38% GM in FY26 (from 36.5% FY25). This *is* in consensus. Not a variant view.

2. **"Walmart shelf reset"** adds ~6% to brand scans. One-time lift fully embedded in FY26.
   - Rejected as primary because it's also in management commentary and consensus. Sell-side $708M FY26 revenue forecast (+16% from $610M) clearly accommodates this.

3. **"International acceleration"** beyond consensus. International grew 37% in FY25; sell-side may be modeling 25-30% going forward.
   - Rejected as primary because it points the wrong way (bullish, not variant). And the sell-side has been on every earnings call hearing about international — they know.

4. **"Private label rebuild"** beyond +20-25% guidance. Several new wins materializing.
   - Rejected as primary because materiality is small (PL = ~10% of revenue; even +35% beats only ~$10M of revenue).

5. **"Multiple compression as growth decelerates."** Selected.

## Why multiple compression is the strongest candidate

- **Quantifiable from peers.** CELH compressed from >50x to 16x as growth normalized. BRBR/SMPL trade at 9-10x. The historical evidence is direct.
- **Implicit in consensus, not explicit.** No published sell-side note appears to explicitly model multiple compression — they show point-estimate target prices that imply the multiple held flat. Reading consensus = 22x exit is an inference, not a stated assumption. That's exactly where mispricings live.
- **Has a clear path to validate or invalidate.** Watch FY26-27 EBITDA growth vs. the implied stock price. If EBITDA grows 25% in FY26 and the stock is flat, multiple is compressing to right level and the variant view is confirmed. If the stock grows with EBITDA, the multiple is holding and the variant view is wrong.
- **Differentiated from consensus.** Sell-side is 4 Strong Buy / 3 Buy / 2 Hold / 0 Sell. A bearish-lean view from the buy-side sits in genuine non-consensus territory.

## What the variant view is *not*

- Not a fundamentals call. We agree with management/consensus on FY26 revenue and EBITDA.
- Not a "company is bad" view. Vita Coco is a high-quality business with a clear growth runway.
- Not a "short the stock" call. The expected -9% return is not a SELL bar; it's a HOLD with bearish lean. Asymmetric risk/reward is genuinely two-sided.

## What would invalidate the variant view

- International maintains 35%+ growth through FY27 → the high-growth premium is justified, multiple holds at 22x, consensus is right.
- Margin expands to 40%+ structurally → the platform-multiple thesis works, multiple holds.
- Major M&A that meaningfully expands the addressable market → multiple expands rather than compresses.

## Consequences

- The memo leads with the variant view in the third paragraph and develops it as the central frame.
- The recommendation is HOLD, not SELL, because the variant view leaves real bull optionality.
- Future revisions of this analysis should track multiple-compression progress quarter by quarter. If by Q3 2026 the stock hasn't declined despite EBITDA expansion, the thesis is weakening. If the stock holds $50-55 while EBITDA goes from $98M to $125M, multiple is mechanically compressing toward the right level — variant view materializing.
