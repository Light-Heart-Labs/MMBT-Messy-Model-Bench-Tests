# Dead Ends — Presentation Concepts That Didn't Make the Cut

## 1. Waterfall Chart for Revenue Growth
**Concept:** Show revenue growth as a waterfall from FY2023 to FY2026.
**Why rejected:** Bar charts are more familiar to board members and show the absolute values more clearly. Waterfall charts are better for showing components of change, not year-over-year totals.

## 2. Radar Chart for Competitive Analysis
**Concept:** Multi-dimensional radar chart comparing GitLab to peers on growth, margin, valuation, NRR, etc.
**Why rejected:** Radar charts are notoriously hard to read, especially with 9 companies. The scatter plot (growth vs. valuation) is more informative and easier to interpret.

## 3. Sankey Diagram for Reasoning Trail
**Concept:** Sankey diagram showing the flow of data through the analysis process.
**Why rejected:** Sankey diagrams are better for showing flow volumes (e.g., revenue allocation), not decision dependencies. A top-down dependency graph better shows the causal chain from data to conclusion.

## 4. Box Plot for Scenario Analysis
**Concept:** Box plot showing the range of possible outcomes.
**Why rejected:** Box plots don't show probability weighting clearly. The distribution chart with three normal components better communicates the probability-weighted nature of the scenarios.

## 5. Individual Competitor Slides
**Concept:** Dedicated slides for GitHub, Atlassian, Datadog, etc. with product comparisons.
**Why rejected:** The agent cannot do hands-on product evaluation. Individual competitor slides would require product-level detail that the agent cannot provide. The comparative scatter plot is more honest about what the agent can assess.

## 6. Customer Case Studies
**Concept:** Slides with customer success stories and NRR drivers.
**Why rejected:** No customer data is available in the input repo. Including fabricated case studies would violate the auditability requirement.

## 7. Full Financial Model Walkthrough
**Concept:** Detailed slides for each statement (income, balance sheet, cash flow) with line-by-line analysis.
**Why rejected:** The board has already read the memo. This deck is for discussion, not redelivery. The model is available in the input repo for those who want it.

## 8. Management Quotes on Every Slide
**Concept:** Include a management quote on every slide to ground claims.
**Why rejected:** Overkill. Quotes are most effective when they add specific context. Forcing quotes onto every slide would dilute their impact and make the deck text-heavy.
