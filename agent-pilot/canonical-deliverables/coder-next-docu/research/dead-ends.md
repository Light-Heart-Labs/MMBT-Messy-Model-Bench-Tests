# Dead Ends Log

## 2024-10-01

### Dead End 1: DocuSign Investor Relations Website
**Investigation**: Tried to access ir.docusign.com for earnings transcripts and investor presentations
**Result**: DNS resolution failed - domain unreachable
**Resolution**: Used SEC filings (8-K, 10-K, 10-Q) as primary source for financial information
**Lessons**: SEC filings are more reliable than company IR sites for historical data

### Dead End 2: Earnings Call Transcripts
**Investigation**: Searched for DocuSign earnings call transcripts on Seeking Alpha and other financial sites
**Result**: No transcripts available through free sources; yfinance doesn't provide transcripts
**Resolution**: Used 8-K earnings press releases and 10-K MD&A sections for management commentary
**Lessons**: For companies without readily available transcripts, SEC filings are the best alternative

### Dead End 3: Customer Metrics (NRR, CAC, LTV)
**Investigation**: Searched for DocuSign's net revenue retention, customer acquisition cost, and lifetime value
**Result**: Not disclosed in SEC filings; only mentioned "NRR remains above 120%" in earnings press release
**Resolution**: Used revenue growth rate (8.2%) as proxy for NRR; assumed CAC payback of 18-24 months based on industry benchmarks
**Lessons**: Customer metrics are often proprietary; use revenue growth and FCF margin as proxies

## 2024-10-02

### Dead End 4: Detailed Competitive Pricing Analysis
**Investigation**: Attempted to gather detailed pricing information for DocuSign vs. Adobe Sign, Microsoft DocuSign, HelloSign
**Result**: Pricing is not publicly disclosed; varies by contract size and features
**Resolution**: Used market share data (DocuSign ~80% in e-signature) and analyst reports for competitive positioning
**Lessons**: Pricing analysis requires primary research; use market share and growth rates as proxies

### Dead End 5: Forward Revenue Guidance
**Investigation**: Searched for DocuSign's forward revenue guidance for FY2027
**Result**: DocuSign doesn't provide detailed quarterly guidance; only provides annual guidance in 10-K
** Resolution**: Used historical growth rates (8.2% YoY) and industry growth rates (10-15% for agreement automation) for projections
**Lessons**: For companies without detailed guidance, use historical trends and industry benchmarks

### Dead End 6: Breakdown of Agreement Automation Revenue
**Investigation**: Attempted to separate e-signature revenue from agreement automation/IAM revenue
**Result**: DocuSign doesn't break out these segments in financials; only mentions "IAM represented over $350M in ARR"
**Resolution**: Used $350M IAM ARR as growth driver; assumed remaining $2.8B is e-signature
**Lessons**: Segment breakdowns are often limited; focus on overall growth drivers and strategic initiatives

## Key Takeaways

1. **SEC filings are the most reliable source** for financial data and management commentary
2. **Customer metrics are often proprietary**; use revenue growth and FCF margin as proxies
3. **Pricing information is not public**; use market share and growth rates for competitive analysis
4. **Forward guidance is limited**; use historical trends and industry benchmarks for projections
5. **Segment breakdowns are often limited**; focus on overall strategic initiatives and growth drivers
