## Executive Summary

DocuSign (DOCU) is the market leader in e-signature with ~80% market share, now expanding into the $20B+ agreement automation and IAM markets. The company generated $3.22B in revenue (+8.2% YoY) and $1.06B in free cash flow (32.9% margin) in FY2026. While revenue growth has slowed from historical rates, DocuSign's transition to an AI-native IAM platform positions it for renewed growth. We recommend a **BUY** with a 12-month price target of $72.00 (30% upside), based on DCF analysis and EV/Revenue multiples.

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Execution Risk** (Medium): Failure to expand beyond e-signature into agreement automation
   - **Mitigation**: Strong product roadmap, Lexion acquisition, AI investments

2. **Competition Risk** (High): Adobe, Microsoft, HelloSign competing in e-signature
   - **Mitigation**: Network effects, brand recognition, integration ecosystem

3. **Working Capital Risk** (Medium): Current ratio of 0.73 indicates liquidity concerns
   - **Mitigation**: Contract liabilities provide funding; strong FCF generation

4. **Integration Risk** (Medium): Lexion acquisition integration challenges
   - **Mitigation**: Management experience, phased integration approach

5. **Valuation Risk** (Low): Current valuation may be too high for slower growth

## Confidence and Limitations

### What We're Sure About

1. **Market Leadership**: DocuSign is the clear leader in e-signature with 80%+ share
2. **Cash Flow Generation**: 32.9% FCF margin is industry-leading and sustainable
3. **TAM Opportunity**: $20-25B TAM in agreement automation and IAM
4. **Financial Data**: All numbers traceable to SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K)

### What We're Guessing

1. **Growth Acceleration**: Agreement automation revenue acceleration is projected, not confirmed
2. **Integration Success**: Lexion integration success is assumed but not guaranteed
3. **Competitive Response**: Adobe/Microsoft response to DocuSign's expansion is uncertain
4. **Working Capital**: Current ratio concerns may persist or worsen

### What a Human Analyst Would Do Differently

1. **Primary Research**: Conduct customer interviews to validate agreement automation demand
2. **Competitive Intelligence**: Gather pricing and product information from competitors
3. **Management Assessment**: Meet with DocuSign leadership to assess execution capability
4. **Industry Expertise**: Leverage industry contacts for market size and growth estimates
5. **Risk Assessment**: More nuanced assessment of competitive threats and execution risks

### Model Limitations

1. **DCF Sensitivity**: Valuation highly sensitive to growth and margin assumptions
2. **Working Capital**: Current ratio of 0.73 not fully captured in DCF
3. **One-Time Items**: FY2025 tax benefits distort year-over-year comparisons
4. **Segment Breakdown**: Limited visibility into e-signature vs. agreement automation revenue
