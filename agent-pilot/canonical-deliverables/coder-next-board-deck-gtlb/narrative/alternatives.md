# Alternative Narrative Structures Considered

**Date:** June 18, 2025  
**Status:** Rejected

---

## Alternative 1: Problem-Solution-Value (Startup Pitch Style)

**Structure:**
1. The Problem: Fragmented DevOps tools
2. GitLab's Solution: Single application
3. Market Opportunity: $30B TAM
4. Traction: Revenue growth, ARR
5. Team: Leadership
6. Financials: Projections
7. Investment Thesis: Why now
8. Recommendation

**Why Rejected:**
- Too startup-focused; board doesn't need "pitch" format
- Hides the recommendation until slide 8
- Doesn't emphasize the reasoning trail enough
- Doesn't show dead ends or verification mechanism

---

## Alternative 2: Deep-Dive Financial Model First

**Structure:**
1. Financial Model Overview
2. Historical Performance
3. Projections and Assumptions
4. DCF Analysis
5. Comparable Company Analysis
6. Scenario Analysis
7. Investment Thesis
8. Risk Assessment
9. Recommendation

**Why Rejected:**
- Leads with technical details; board may lose interest
- Doesn't tell a compelling story
- Doesn't show the agent's reasoning process
- Doesn't address skepticism about the model

---

## Alternative 3: Question-Driven Narrative

**Structure:**
1. Is GitLab undervalued? (Yes)
2. Why is it undervalued? (Market missing AI potential)
3. How do we know? (DCF + EV/Revenue)
4. What could go wrong? (Risks)
5. How do we verify? (Audit trail)

**Why Rejected:**
- Too abstract; lacks concrete data early
- Doesn't lead with the recommendation
- Doesn't show the full competitive landscape
- Doesn't demonstrate the reasoning trail visually

## Alternative 6: Risk-First Approach (Bear Case First)

**Structure:**
1. Key Risks (NRR decline, competition, macro)
2. Mitigation Strategies
3. Investment Thesis
4. Financials
5. Valuation

**Why Rejected:**

---

## Selected Structure: Recommendation-First with Reasoning Trail

**Why This Works:**
1. **Leads with conclusion** - Board members are busy; they need to know the recommendation first
2. **Shows reasoning** - Technical board wants to evaluate the agent's process, not just the result
3. **Demonstrates thoroughness** - Shows dead ends and how we adapted
4. **Enables verification** - Every claim is traceable to source
5. **Balances data and narrative** - Charts tell the story; text provides context

**Key Differentiators:**
- Slide 1: Recommendation (what we think)
- Slide 3: Thesis (why we think it)
- Slide 11: Reasoning trail (how we got there)
- Slide 12: Dead ends (what we ruled out)
- Slide 13: Verification (how to check our work)

This structure is designed for a skeptical, technical audience that wants to evaluate the reasoning, not just rubber-stamp a recommendation.
