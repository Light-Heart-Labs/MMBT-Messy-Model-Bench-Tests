# Storyboard: GitLab (GTLB) Board Presentation

## Narrative Arc

**Opening → Thesis → Evidence → Valuation → Risks → Process → Verification**

The board has read the memo. They know the recommendation is BUY at $42. This deck is not about convincing them to buy GitLab — it's about convincing them that the *agent system* that made this recommendation is reliable, transparent, and rigorous. Every slide serves dual purpose: present the investment case AND demonstrate the reasoning trail.

## Slide-by-Slide Storyboard

### Act I: The Recommendation (Slides 1-3) — 5 min

**Slide 1: Title & Recommendation**
- GitLab (GTLB), BUY, $42 target, 95% upside
- Confidence meter: 7/10
- Current price: $21.51
- *Why first:* Lead with the answer. Board members want to know the recommendation immediately.

**Slide 2: The Thesis in One Slide**
- 5 bullet points, agent's own words from memo
- Revenue growth + margin improvement + AI monetization + balance sheet + mispricing
- *Why second:* The "what" before the "why." Board needs the thesis to evaluate the evidence.

**Slide 3: What Would Change the Recommendation**
- 3 conditions that would flip BUY to HOLD/SELL
- NRR < 110%, GitHub Copilot eats share, macro recession
- *Why third:* Shows intellectual honesty. If the board can't see the exit, they won't trust the entry.

### Act II: The Evidence (Slides 4-8) — 10 min

**Slide 4: Financial Trajectory**
- Revenue, operating margin, FCF, ARR — all on one chart
- Inflection points called out (FCF positive FY2024, margin inflection)
- Historical + projected with confidence bands
- *Why fourth:* The numbers are the foundation. Show the trend, not just the snapshot.

**Slide 5: Competitive Position**
- Scatter plot: growth vs. margin (not a pie chart — pie obscures the thesis)
- GitLab positioned vs. 8 comps
- Why these comps: SaaS infrastructure, similar growth profiles
- *Why fifth:* Competitive context. The scatter reveals that GitLab is the only large-cap SaaS at 2.6x EV/Revenue with 23% growth.

**Slide 6: The Mispricing Thesis**
- Why the market is wrong: AI monetization not priced in, operating leverage trajectory, balance sheet optionality
- Sell-side consensus ($30.79) vs. our target ($42)
- *Why sixth:* This is the core of the investment case. The market sees unprofitability; we see the path to it.

**Slide 7: Risk Assessment**
- Risk matrix: probability vs. impact
- Each risk with "what would have to be true" condition
- *Why seventh:* Boards respect risk-aware analysts. Show we've thought about what could go wrong.

### Act III: The Valuation (Slides 8-10) — 5 min

**Slide 8: Bear/Base/Bull Scenarios**
- Probability-weighted distribution visualization (not three bullet points)
- Show the price target as a distribution, not a single number
- *Why eighth:* Valuation is inherently uncertain. Show the uncertainty, don't hide it.

**Slide 9: DCF Bridge**
- How we get from $21.51 to $42: revenue growth → margin expansion → FCF → DCF
- Key assumptions called out
- *Why ninth:* Makes the valuation mechanical, not magical.

### Act IV: The Process (Slides 10-12) — 5 min

**Slide 10: The Reasoning Trail**
- Dependency graph: filings → analysis → decisions → conclusion
- Shows the actual path the agent took
- *Why tenth:* This is the capability demonstration. How did we get here?

**Slide 11: Dead Ends**
- Hypotheses investigated and rejected
- SEC filing failures, CIK confusion, DNS issues
- *Why eleventh:* Boards trust analysts who show their work. Dead ends prove rigor.

**Slide 12: Confidence & Limitations**
- What we're confident about vs. what we're estimating
- Visual: confidence thermometer or radar chart
- *Why twelfth:* Honest assessment of uncertainty.

### Act V: Verification (Slides 13-15) — 5 min

**Slide 13: How to Audit This Deck**
- Step-by-step: clone repo, pick any claim, follow trace
- Shows the audit trail mechanism
- *Why thirteenth:* The most important slide for a skeptical technical board.

**Slide 14: Number Reconciliation**
- 5 random numbers from the deck, fully reconciled
- *Why fourteenth:* Proof that the audit mechanism works.

**Slide 15: Q&A / Discussion**
- Open floor
- *Why fifteenth:* The deck is for discussion, not redelivery.

## Visual Design Principles

1. **Charts are the visual centerpiece** — no clip-art, no stock imagery
2. **Color consistency** — bear scenarios = red, bull = green, base = blue across all slides
3. **Sector colors** — GitLab = teal, each competitor gets a consistent color
4. **Sequential colormaps** — for the financial trajectory, because the data has a natural zero point
5. **No AI-generated faces** — all imagery is data-driven

## Timing

- 30 min presentation + 15 min Q&A
- Act I: 5 min (fast, get to the point)
- Act II: 10 min (the meat — evidence)
- Act III: 5 min (valuation)
- Act IV: 5 min (process — the capability demo)
- Act V: 5 min (verification — the trust builder)

## Rejected Narrative Structures

1. **Chronological approach** (data → analysis → conclusion) — Rejected because the board has read the memo. They want discussion, not a lecture.
2. **Risk-first approach** — Rejected because it frames the investment as defensive. We want to lead with the opportunity.
3. **Competitive analysis first** — Rejected because the competitive context is secondary to the financial trajectory.
4. **Valuation first** — Rejected because the valuation is only meaningful in the context of the thesis.
